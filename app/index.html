Cyberpunk re-imagined for

2021

> A tech/cyberpunk magazine called Mondo 2000 published an issue in 1993 that featured a look into the gear that a proper cyberpunk would wear in 2000, called “R.U.Cyberpunk”. Now that cyberpunk is vogue again and technology has progressed slightly from MiniDisc players and luggable Apple Powerbooks, we’ve decided to try to modernize the tech to the year 2021 (yes, face mask included).

THE HARDWARE

THE USUAL

The basic hardware in every cyberpunk’s arsenal in 2021 should be a good phone and a good laptop. And we prefer things where we can unlock, install the things we want, but still keep them as inconspicuous as possible.

  • Phones
    • Samsung Z Fold 2 5G
    • Moto Razr 2019

  • Laptops
    • Thinkpad X1 Carbon G8
    • Macbook Air 2020

As you’ll soon see, we’re going to be plugging in a lot of gear to the phone and laptop and having the necessary adapters and peripherals are key. After all, a piece of equipment is useless if you can’t plug it in or it’s out of juice. So consider the following:


  • Multifunction USB hub (USB-A/C, HDMI, card readers, etc)
    • Aukey C71
    • UGreen 9-in-1 USB C Hub

  • Powerbanks
    • Aukey Sprint Ultra 30000mAh Power Bank with 30W Power Delivery & Quick Charge 3.0

  • Input devices
    • Indiscreet: Jelly Comb Dual Mode Bluetooth Folding Keyboard or Ultimate Hacking Keyboard
    • Jelly Comb Dual Mode Bluetooth Folding Keyboard
    • Ultimate Hacking Keyboard
    • Discreet: Tap Strap 2, ArcX ring

Being unconnected is pretty much not an option today, so having good connectivity is paramount, even if you’re stuck in a remote location. Sometimes satellite is the only connectivity you get, and sometimes you really need the bandwidth:


  • Iridium GO
  • Peplink Pepwave MAX HD4 MBX

OFFENSIVE

Back in the days, you’d might need to carry around multiple transceivers and modems to listen or transmit different frequency ranges. Everything has gotten easier with the advent of Software Defined Radios, which can be highly portable and connect via standard USB interface. The magic is now all in the software.

  • HackRF One
  • LimeSDR

Since WiFi and Bluetooth networks are so ubiquitous nowadays, you may want some dedicated hardware to poke around them. IoT also has given rise to some less known wireless networking technologies, such as Zigbee or LoRaWAN.

  • WiFi: WiFi Pineapple
  • Pwnagotchi
  • Bluetooth: Ubertooth One
  • Zigbee: TI SmartRF Protocol Packet Sniffer

SDR radios are still bound by physical limitations, so you’ll still need an antenna though. Since most of us don’t fancy carrying around a big spool of antenna cable, we’ll try to select couple of omnidirectional and directional antennas for on the go:

  • High Power USB-Yagi Plug and Play directional WiFi Antenna
  • Omnidirectional antenna

Sad state of modern world is that most hardware is not open source and spec sheets might not be readily available, so a modern hardware hacker will need a suite of tools to inspect and hack hardware. And often it’s best if that gear is smaller than a SEMS microscope.
Sometimes it might not be easy to actually find the hardware you’re looking for. Maybe it’s hidden behind a wall or maybe you need to find the correct server that’s crunching data at high load. You might be using a FLIR thermal camera to look for hot spots. Luckily they are much smaller than before and can be plugged into any phone:

  • FLIR One Gen 3

Most hardware comes enclosed in something and that’s the first barrier to get through. So a good toolkit is in order:

  • Toolkit: Ifixit Pro Tech Toolkit
  • Xiaomi Mijia Wiha Screwdriver set
  • Soldering iron - Pinecil
  • Soldering iron - TS80P
  • Lockpicking set: Multipick Elite Champion

Once the device lays bare, a hacker would need to understand a few basic electronics details - power supply, types of components, types of signals being shuffled around etc. A good multimeter and scope comes handy then. While you can spend a lot of money buying the latest and greatest Fluke gear, we’ve selected some more affordable hardware that should still get most jobs done:

  • Scope: Lab Nation Smart Scope
  • Multimeter: OWON 3 5/6 Digital Multimeter With Bluetooth

Most components operate at too high speeds for a scope to be useful, so we’ll also want some equipment to deal with things on a logic level. Many devices nowadays will have a JTAG interface that allows to inspect the device on a higher level than rising or falling edges of a waveform:

  • Segger J-Link
  • GreatFET
  • Bus Pirate

Sometimes you might need to tap into hardline networks to get the information you need. Tools and skills for those are not trivial, but available to the hardcore enthuasist:

  • Fiber transceivers:
    • Startech USB 3.0 to Fiber Optic Converter - Compact USB to Open SFP Adapter

  • Fiber optic taps:
    • Exfo FCD-10 Fiber Clip-On (discontinued******)

  • Ethernet taps:
    • Profitap ProfiShark 1G

So far we’ve covered hardware, but if you're a serious black hat or dedicated red teamer, you’ll probably want to cover the soft side of things: people. Surveillance is not pretty, but sometimes people are the weakest link. You can use your eyes and ears for that, but since cyber optics are not yet available, we’ll have to make do with the next best things:

  • Zoom attachment for phone
    • Reasonable: Tele 58mm Lens
    • Ridiculous: Apexel 28x Super Zoom Telephoto

  • Flashlight
    • Nitecore CU6
    • Nitecore P20UV
    • Nitecore Mh17UV

To track patterns efficiently while surveilling, you’ll want to make notes. If you’re old fashioned, you’ll probably go with a pen and paper solution:

  • Hacker’s field notes

DEFFENSIVE

Folks, it’s 2021, there’s no excuse to not use Multi Factor Authentication. Because several modes of MFA are vulnerable to phishing attacks (like SMS), the strongest security is achieved by using a physical security key (usually FIDO U2F standard). There are plenty of keys to choose from and some of them even come with biometric checks that provide a little bit of extra security for absent-minded punk:

  • OnlyKey
  • Verimark with fingerprint
  • Solokeysli>
  • OpenSK
  • BioPass FIDO2

Also, you should be using condoms. And by that I mean USB condoms. They prevent your device from getting infected with malware, when you’re plugging in your phone or laptop for charging:

  • PortaPow 3rd Gen USB Data Blocker

Cryptocurrency has really taken off in 2021, with Bitcoin reaching new highs and Dogecoin sounding every day more like a sensible investment. But cryptocurrency is inherently vulnerable and most paranoid cyberpunks would rock a hardware wallet to store their Bitcoins:

  • Ledger Nano X
